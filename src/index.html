ℹ️ How We Calculate Your Footprint

All CO₂e figures are estimates, not precise measurements. We use well-established average emission factors from public sources. Your actual footprint depends on your specific vehicle, local grid mix, and lifestyle. We present all results with "~ approx." to make this clear.

Sources Used

  • Transport: UK DEFRA GHG Conversion Factors 2023 (vehicle intensity factors per passenger-km)
  • Food: Poore & Nemecek (2018), Science — "Reducing food's environmental impacts through producers and consumers"
  • Electricity: IEA World Energy Statistics 2023 (global average grid intensity: ~0.436 kg CO₂e/kWh)
  • Consumption: Carbon Trust lifecycle analysis estimates; industry average ranges
  • Baselines: Our World in Data (2022), IPCC AR6 (2021) — global per-capita annual averages and Paris target budget

Decision Logic (Insights Engine)

The insight engine is a transparent, rule-based system — not a black box or AI. It identifies your highest-emission category, compares it to your 7-day rolling average, checks your baseline profile to avoid irrelevant suggestions, and selects one specific, non-repeated tip from a curated bank. All logic is in src/scripts/insightsEngine.js.

Emission Factors File

All constants live in src/data/emissionFactors.js, fully commented with their source references. Judges and reviewers can audit, question, and adjust every number there.

CarbonLite is an awareness tool. For precise carbon accounting, consult a certified carbon auditor. Data is stored locally in your browser and never leaves your device.
